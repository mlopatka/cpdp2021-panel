Martin Lopatka (mlopatka@mozilla.com) is a senior manager of research engineering at Mozilla's Systems Research Group. Leveraging technologies from the space of machine learning, graph theory, and applied Bayesian statistics, his team explores both fundamental and product-facing questions into the nature of the Web and way we use it. This group has produced several recent works designing the next generation of privacy enhancing technologies and exploring the depths of the worlds largest shared global resource. Martin received his PhD in Statistics from the University of Amsterdam in 2016, and his MSc in Artificial Intelligence in 2009 and has revelled in high dimensional statistical data analysis problems in a variety of application domains.
